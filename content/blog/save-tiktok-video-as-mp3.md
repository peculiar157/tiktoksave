Sometimes it's not the video you're after — it's the sound. Maybe it's a snippet you want for a project, a voiceover you want to reference, or a song you heard on your feed and can't get out of your head. Here's the fastest way to pull the audio out of a TikTok video as a plain MP3 file.

## Why not just screen-record?

Screen recording works in a pinch, but it re-encodes the audio through your device's microphone or system audio pipeline, which almost always drops quality and can pick up background noise. Pulling the audio track directly from the source file gives you the original recording as TikTok stored it — no re-encoding, no quality loss.

## Step-by-step

1. **Find the video** with the sound you want and tap **Share → Copy Link**.
2. **Paste the link into [DLTok](/)** and hit "Get download links."
3. Once the preview loads, click **Download MP3**. The audio track saves directly to your device, ready to drop into a video editor, voice memo app, or music library.

## What you're actually downloading

TikTok stores the audio for a video as a separate track from the visual footage — that's the same track used when someone else "uses this sound" on a different video. When you download the MP3 through DLTok, you're getting that original audio file, not a re-recording of the video's playback.

## A quick legal note

Sounds on TikTok are often licensed music, not original audio from the creator who posted the video. Saving a sound for personal reference (learning it on an instrument, remembering the title, editing your own clip for personal use) is generally fine. Publishing or monetizing someone else's copyrighted song elsewhere is a different matter — that runs into the same copyright rules as sampling music from any other platform. If the sound is an original voiceover or podcast clip from the creator, it's best to credit them or get their permission before reusing it publicly.
