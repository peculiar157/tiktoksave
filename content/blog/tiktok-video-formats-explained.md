When you paste a TikTok link into a downloader, you're usually offered a few different options: MP4, sometimes a separate HD MP4, and MP3. Here's what each one actually means and when to reach for it.

## MP4 (standard)

This is the everyday video file format — the same format used by nearly every phone, editing app, and social platform. When a downloader offers a plain "MP4" option, it's giving you the video track TikTok's page serves by default, which for most videos is already solid quality (TikTok compresses uploads to keep its app fast, so "standard" and "HD" are sometimes close to identical for shorter clips).

**Use it for:** everyday saving, re-sharing, quick edits.

## HD MP4

Some TikTok videos have more than one stored version — TikTok's own player can switch between them depending on your connection speed. When a downloader shows an "HD" option, it's exposing the highest-bitrate version available for that specific video, if one exists. Not every video has a meaningfully different HD version; it depends on how the original was uploaded and encoded.

**Use it for:** longer-form content, videos with a lot of detail or fast motion, or anything you plan to edit further and want the least amount of compression artifacting in.

## MP3

This strips out the video entirely and gives you just the audio track — the same sound used when someone taps "use this sound" on TikTok. It's a real audio file, not a recording of the video playing, so there's no quality loss from the extraction process.

**Use it for:** saving a song or sound bite, referencing a voiceover, or building an audio-only clip library.

## Quick decision guide

- Want to repost or edit the video? **MP4** (or **HD MP4** if it's available and the extra quality matters to you).
- Want just the sound? **MP3**.
- Not sure if HD makes a difference? Download both — HD will never be *worse* than standard, so it's a safe default when it's offered.
